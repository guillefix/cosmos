<p>A more biologically plausible kind of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Artificial%20neural%20network">Artificial neural network</a>, which takes direct inspiration on biologial <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Neuronal%20network">Neuronal network</a>s. It is a model that is the basis for the design of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Neuromorphic%20computing">Neuromorphic computing</a> systems. It models the time-dependent spikes of real <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Neuron">Neuron</a>s. These spikes correspond to <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Action%20potential">Action potential</a> spikes that travel through the neuron's axons, dendrites, and soma (bodies).</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://jackterwilliger.com/biological-neural-networks-part-i-spiking-neurons/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://jackterwilliger.com/biological-neural-networks-part-i-spiking-neurons/</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P6ysScmb9hY&amp;t=279s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">CS-DC'15: From Spikes to Cognitive Agents with Neural Assembly Computing</a></p><p>The first scientific model of a spiking neuron, proposed by Hodgkin and Huxley [21], is based on experimental recordings from the giant squid axon using a voltage clamp method. The complexity in simulating this biologically realistic model is very high due to the number of differential equations and the large number of parameters. Thus, most computer simulations choose to use a simplified neuron model such as the integrate-and-fire model (I&amp;F), leaky I&amp;F model, conductance-based I&amp;F or Izhikevich׳s model. The I&amp;F model simulates the state of the neuron by its membrane potential, which receives excitatory or inhibitory signals from synaptic inputs from other neurons. Each input is weighted by its associated synaptic strength. The leaky I&amp;F model produces a more biologically realistic neuron model adding a “leak” term to the membrane potential, reflecting the diffusion of ions that occurs through the membrane when some equilibrium is not reached in the cell. A full review of the biological behaviour of single neurons can be found in [13] and a comparison of different neuron models can be found in [26].</p><p>Quantification of the spiking is often done by the <strong>instantaneous firing rate</strong>, the number of spikes per unit of time, in average. However, simulating the individual spikes can be useful, because the brain seems to generate spike timing patterns that sometimes give rise to precise <strong>spike-timing dynamics</strong>. One can also choose to model the axonal delays or not. Both spikes and axonal delays are necessary to study the self-organization of polychrony (<a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.izhikevich.org/publications/spnet.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">paper</a>).</p><dl><dd><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Delayed%20dynamical%20system">Delayed dynamical system</a>s can exhibit astonishingly rich and
