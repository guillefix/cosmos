</span> </div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p>The knowledge regarding all the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Science.html">natural aspects</a> and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Art.html">artificial constructs</a> related to Humanity, the collective of the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Human.html">Human species</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Evolution.html">evolved</a> in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Planet%2520Earth.html">Planet Earth</a>.</p><p>We include here what is normally known as <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanities" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">humanities</a>, but also <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Social%2520sciences.html">Social sciences</a> that treat aspects of Humanity <sub>(so for examples scientific studies of animal societies are not part of humanities, although part of social sciences)</sub></p><p>Although humans are the origin of our currently known complex social systems, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Transhumanism.html">transhuman advancements</a> (like the development of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Artificial%2520intelligence.html">AI</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Mind%2520uploading.html">Mind uploading</a>, or <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Genetic%2520engineering.html">Genetic engineering</a>), or the discovery of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Extraterrestrial%2520life.html">Extraterrestrial life</a> make may future <em>non-human</em> agents as, or even more important, in society. Cosmos will need to then be upgraded with a new term more encompassing than &quot;Humanities&quot;. Society is one candidate for such a general term, and indeed <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Social%2520sciences.html">Social sciences</a> have gone beyond standard <em>humanities</em> in studying social aspects of non-humans. In any case, our current social systems are still mostly human-centered, and the centrality of this tiddler represents that state of affairs.</p><p>Note that even as animal right movements are succeeding in giving animals fundamental rights of living and sentient beings (as the right to be protected from suffering), animals will probably still play a secondary role in society, as humans are generally more complex and intelligent in their behaviour.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m6ugFcwsgpE" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Carl Sagan: We Humans Are Capable Of Greatness</a></p><h3 class=""><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dwXqoaThGvA" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Wanderers (Carl Sagan)</a></u></h3><hr><h2 class=""><u>Humans</u></h2><p>The study of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Human.html">Human</a>s per se (whether individually, or collectively in societies) is called <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Anthropology.html">Anthropology</a>. </p><p>Humans are part of the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Tree%2520of%2520life.html">Tree of life</a>, and their natural aspects are thus studied in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Biology.html">Biology</a>, in particular in <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_anthropology" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">biological anthropology</a>. On the other hand, the collective of artificial constructs created by Humanity is known as <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Culture.html">Culture</a> (studied by <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/cultural_anthropology" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">cultural anthropology</a>).</p><h2 class=""><u>Human society</u></h2><p>Humans organize themselves in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Social%2520sciences.html">societies</a>. The organization often involves systems of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Law.html">Law</a> (~what can* be done), <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Politics.html">Politics</a> (~what should we do), and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Economics.html">Economics</a> (~how to do we get what we need).</p><p><sub>* what can be done, here of course refers to not just what can be done by physical laws, but what is permitted by <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Law.html">Law</a>, the societal construct that dictates what humans are allowed or not to do, by society</sub></p><p>Physical aspects of these societies are mostly studied in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Geography.html">Geography</a>, particularly in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Human%2520geography.html">Human geography</a>.</p><p>Human communication is a crucial aspect of the human condition, and of the resulting societies. It is studied in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Linguistics.html">Linguistics</a> (and in particular, for humans, in <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linguistic_anthropology" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">linguistic anthropology</a>)</p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
