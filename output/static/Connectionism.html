roughly the same time as artificial intelligence, all deeply influenced by the advent of the computer (Chomsky, 1956) (Miller, 2003). Their intentions were to explain human mental behaviour based on information or symbol-processing metaphors. In the early 1980s, both fields considered recursive or procedural (rule-based) symbol-processing to be the highest mark of cognition. The Parallel Distributed Processing (PDP) or connectionist revolution cast aside the symbol-processing metaphor in favour of a so-called “sub-symbolic” description of thought processes (Rumelhart et al., 1986)</p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
