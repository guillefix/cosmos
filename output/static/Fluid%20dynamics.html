<p><strong>Fluid dynamics</strong> is the branch of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fluid%20mechanics">Fluid mechanics</a> that describes the causes of motion, i.e. the forces and torques that can affect fluids, and how these affect their motion. </p><p>The equations of fluid dynamics can be derived from the principles of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Mechanics">Mechanics</a> (in particular continuum mechanics). More recently they have also been derived from the microscopic statistical picture of moving and interacting particles thanks to the development of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Kinetic%20theory">Kinetic theory</a>.</p><p><strong>Navier-Stokes equation</strong> </p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navier%E2%80%93Stokes_equations" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navier%E2%80%93Stokes_equations</a></p><p>Oxford course.
