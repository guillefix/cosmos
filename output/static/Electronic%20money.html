<p>Electronic money (e-money) is electronically (including magnetically) stored monetary value, represented by a claim on the issuer, which is issued on receipt of funds for the purpose of making payment transactions. It is accepted by a person other than the electronic money issuer.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.fca.org.uk/firms/electronic-money-regulations" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">FCA</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.google.co.uk/url?sa=t&amp;source=web&amp;rct=j&amp;url=https://www.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/370910/Positive_Money_response.pdf&amp;ved=0ahUKEwjvzurhoqLPAhWTSxoKHUKBDCcQFggrMAU&amp;usg=AFQjCNFmlS96LYrB6W-Yosc4vomGwdqtYg&amp;sig2=hfetsDg8nEjnqjNRFuz9fw" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Positive money - Electronic money issuers</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://transferwise.com" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://transferwise.com</a></p>
