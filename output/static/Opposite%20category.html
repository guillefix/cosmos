The opposite of a category CCC, written CopC^{op}C​op​​, has the same objects and arrows as CCC, but the arrows are reversed in direction (domains become codomains, and viceversa):

f:A→Bf: A \to Bf:A→B in CopC^{op}C​op​​ ⇔\Leftrightarrow⇔ f:B→Af: B \to Af:B→A in CCC.

Composition, f∘gf \circ gf∘g in CopC^{op}C​op​​ is defined as the arrow g∘fg \circ fg∘f in CCC (which remember, points in the opposite direction. Direction is defined with the dom,coddom, coddom,cod maps, so they are separate from the arrows themselves..)

This allows us to describe a duality principle:

If we have an statement ϕ\phiϕ, which holds in CCC. Then, the same statement, but with arrows reversed (and compositions flipped) ϕ∗\phi^*ϕ​∗​​ holds in CopC^{op}C​op​​.

In particular CopC^{op}C​op​​ is a category, because the axioms of a category are self-dual.

In this sense, monic and epic are dual.
