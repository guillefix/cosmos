</span> </div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p>The term &quot;perceptron&quot; was introduced in the 1950s to designate a simple mechanism to achieve &quot;perception.&quot;</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HZ4cvaztQEs&amp;index=3&amp;list=PLA89DCFA6ADACE599#t=1h10m" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">video</a>. <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_q5twKE9Jeo#t=14m20s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Definition of perceptron</a> </p><p>It is basically a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Feedforward%2520neural%2520network.html">Feedforward neural network</a>, with 0 hidden layers, and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Heaviside%2520step%2520function.html">Heaviside step function</a> activation functions. They are a simpler version of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Logistic%2520regression.html">Logistic regression</a>.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Perceptron" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Perceptron</a></p><p>As most older models, the original perceptrons, had threshold activation functions (see <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Hopfield%2520network.html">Hopfield network</a>).</p><p>The hopes to build &quot;<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Computer%2520vision.html">seeing machines</a>&quot; vanished when Minsky and Papert published their book Perceptrons [1969], in which they rigorously demonstrated that perceptrons are quite limited in their ability to extract global features from local information. They could only implement linearly separable classification.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_q5twKE9Jeo#t=15m50s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Simple storing problem</a>: is a certain training set linearly separable?</p><h2 class=""><u>Multilayer perceptron</u></h2><p>However, the reaction was too drastic, because adding more layers to the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Feedforward%2520neural%2520network.html">Feedforward neural network</a> (giving so-called &quot;multilayer perceptrons&quot;), avoided the issues pointed out by Minksy and Papert.</p><p>Multilayered perceptrons work essentially in a manner similar to the prevailing neurophysiological view. According to this view, on arrival at the cortex, sensory information is subject to a hierarchy of feature extractions. Further comparison with the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Cortex.html">Cortex</a>, is done in the Corticonics book (pages 200-203)</p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
