tc-reveal"><p>An <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Active%2520system.html">active</a> particle, often a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Colloid.html">colloid</a>, or a nanoparticle, that propels itself through a fluid, often via some <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Phoretic%2520mechanisms%2520of%2520colloids.html">phoretic mechanism</a>, or via some mechanical propulsion mechanism (the particles are then often called <strong>microswimmers</strong>). <sub>Generally, &quot;active colloid&quot; simply refers to a self-propelled colloid (and similarly with &quot;active particle&quot; in general).</sub></p><p>&quot;In the current miniaturization race towards small motors and engines, a rapidly expanding subdomain is the quest for autonomous swimmers, able to move in fluids which appear very viscous given the small length scales (low Reynolds number). <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Robotic%2520microswimmer.html">Robotic microswimmer</a>s that generate surface distortions is an avenue (e.g. by mimicking sperms [1]), but it seems equally interesting to try to take advantage of physical phenomena that become predominant at small scales. Interfacial <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Phoretic%2520mechanisms%2520of%2520colloids.html">‘phoretic’ effects</a> (electrophoresis, thermophoresis, diffusiophoresis, [2]) by which the gradients of fields (electrostatic potential, temperature, concentration) drive the motion of colloid particles, are from this standpoint a natural avenue given the increased surface to volume ratio of smaller objects. &quot;</p><h2 class=""><u>Robotic microswimmers</u></h2><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/v437/n7060/full/nature04090.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Microscopic artificial swimmers</a></p><h2 class=""><u>Phoretic swimmers</u></h2><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Designing%2520phoretic%2520micro-%2520and%2520nano-swimmers.html">Designing phoretic micro- and nano-swimmers</a>.
