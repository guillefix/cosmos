tc-reveal"><p>Manners are a set of customs used to restrict our behaviour so as to make others feel more comfortable, expecting they will also do so.</p><p>In Spain, at least, this is called <em>educacion</em> / politeness.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JCTzbc76WXY" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">video</a></p><p>French queen Eleanor of Aquitaine initiates the cultural meme (known as cavalry) that sets the manners of men around women: showing them respect, etc.</p><p><img src="http://revistabulevard.ro/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/mind-your-manners.jpg"></p><p>The invention of a certain kind of delicate glassware in Italy also meant an expression that controlling our force was seen as noble/civilized.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Jean-Jacques_Rousseau" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Russeau</a> challenged our view of manners as something which good/the basis of our civilization. His extreme concept of the <em>noble savage</em> proclaimed the opposite: that being relaxed, unrestrained is how we feel better/happier. His views still resonate in modern times, and have helped reach a balance between the high class of XVIII century France, and the totally wild behaviour of our prehistoric ancestors.</p><p>America was an early hotbed for the evolution of modern manners, as it was a new country.</p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
