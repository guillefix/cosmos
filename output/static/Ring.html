Introduction

A ring is a nonemtpy Set RRR together with two Binary operations:

Addition. +:R×R→R+: R \times R \to R+:R×R→R. (a,b)↦a+b(a,b) \mapsto a+b(a,b)↦a+b
Multiplication. ⋅:R×R→R\cdot : R \times R \to R⋅:R×R→R. (a,b)↦a⋅b(a,b) \mapsto a \cdot b(a,b)↦a⋅b

and an element 0∈R0 \in R0∈R, such that

  • (R,+)(R,+)(R,+) is a commutative group with neutral element 000 (like the Integers with addition, for e.g.). We use the same notation as for standard Addition.
  • ⋅\cdot⋅ is associative. (a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)(a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c)(a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)
  • Distributive law (compatibility): a⋅(b+c)=a⋅b+a⋅ca \cdot (b+c) = a\cdot b + a \cdot ca⋅(b+c)=a⋅b+a⋅c and (b+c)⋅=b⋅a+c⋅a(b+c) \cdot = b \cdot a + c \cdot a(b+c)⋅=b⋅a+c⋅a

(R,+)(R,+)(R,+) is called the Additive group of RRR. 000 is also called the zero element of RRR. For notation, we can also omit ⋅\cdot⋅, and it is implied.

Example: (Z,+,⋅)(\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)(Z,+,⋅) (the Integers).
