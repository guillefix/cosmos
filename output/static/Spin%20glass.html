So, in an alloy like copper manganese, it might be suspected that these conduction electrons are playing some role. And that suspicion is correct.</p><p>Electron spins have two properties that are crucial to their mediation role:</p><ul><li>The first is that electrons carry their own intrinsic magnetic moments. This means that the <strong>magnetic moments of the conduction electrons can interact with those of the localized moments</strong> on the manganese atoms, for example, through mutual spin flips as an electron passes by the manganese moment.</li><li>The second is that, as <strong>quantum mechanical</strong> objects, electrons travel through metals as waves, meaning that, like all waves, electrons can exhibit <strong>diffraction and interference</strong>. As conduction electrons zip past and interact with the localized moment, one gets concentric spheres, centered on the localized manganese moment, of conduction electron spins polarized parallel and antiparallel to the localized moment.
