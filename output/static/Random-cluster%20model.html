A family of Probabilistic models invented by Fortuin and Kasteleyn which include Percolation, and the Ising and Potts models as special cases.

The configuration space of the random-cluster model is the set of all subsets of the edge-set EEE, which we represent as the set Ω=0,1E\Omega={0,1}^EΩ=0,1​E​​. The model may be viewed asa parametric family of probability measures ϕp,q\phi_{p,q}ϕ​p,q​​ on Ω\OmegaΩ. When q=1q=1q=1, we recover bond Percolation, when q=2q=2q=2, we have the Ising model, and when q=2,3,4,...q=2,3,4,...q=2,3,4,... we have different versions of the Potts model.

It turns out that long-range order in a Potts model corresponds to theexistence of infinite clusters in the corresponding random-cluster model. In this sense the Potts and percolation phase transitions are counterparts of one another.
