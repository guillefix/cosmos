<p><strong>Induction</strong>, or inductive reasoning, or inductive inference, refers to infering general models from specific observations/facts/data, which usually exhibit some regularities, properties, or relations.</p><p>Another definition: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yXkEpzexYIU#t=8m38s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">&quot;inference about the unknown by extrapolating a patter about the known&quot;</a>. He also discusses justifying the use of induction. <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yXkEpzexYIU#t=13m20s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Part of the reason induction is useful is the</a> <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Simplicity">Simplicity</a> of the world.</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Universal%20inductive%20inference">Universal inductive inference</a>, for a formalization of optimal inductive inference.</p><p><u>Principles</u></p><ul><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Occam's%20razor">Occam's razor</a></li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Baye's%20theorem">Baye's theorem</a></li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Principle%20of%20indifference">Principle of indifference</a></li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Principle%20of%20multiple%20explanations">Principle of multiple explanations</a></li></ul><p>See more at <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Universal%20inductive%20inference">Universal inductive inference</a></p><p>See also <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Deduction">Deduction</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Transduction">Transduction</a></p>
