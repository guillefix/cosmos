</div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Sound.html">Sound</a>s produced by <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Nature.html">Nature</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s-6CvmmcG0w" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">The Sounds Of Earth</a></p><p>Most sounds we perceive are transmitted through <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Air.html">Air</a>, the medium in which we normally live. These are produced whenever vibrations are excited in the air. Because these require motion, it makes sense that most sounds in Nature are due to the more dynamical aspects of it: <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Life.html">Life</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Fluid.html">Fluid</a>s</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Ethology.html">Ethology</a> for the immense variety sounds that <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Animal.html">Animal</a>s produce.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Fluid.html">Fluid</a>s include <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Liquid.html">Liquid</a>s and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Gas.html">Gas</a>es. The most prominent liquid is water, and indeed the interaction of water with gases (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Bubble.html">Bubble</a>s), itself (waterfall, waves on the sea), or with <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Solid.html">Solid</a> matter (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Rain.html">Rain</a> falling on the ground, or <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Leaf.html">leaves</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Pebble.html">Pebble</a>s rolling in a stream, etc.) are among the most common non-biological natural sounds.</p><p>The complex interaction with air with solid surfaces can also produce the recognizable sounds of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Wind.html">Wind</a>, sometimes producing sounds with a relatively clear pitch, like of air flowing through imperfect organ pipes.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Electricity.html">Electrical</a> phenomena, such as thunder, produce remarkable sounds where the air is directly stimulated by its sudden ionization.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Rock.html">Rock</a>s falling downhill, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Volcano.html">volcanic</a> eruptions, earthquakes, etc.</p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
