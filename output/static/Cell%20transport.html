<p><strong>Cell transport</strong> is movement of materials across cell membranes. Cell transport includes passive and active transport:</p><ul><li>Passive transport does not require energy</li><li>Active transport requires energy to proceed. </li></ul><p>Passive transport proceeds through (simple) diffusion, facilitated diffusion and osmosis.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0034-4885/74/11/116601/meta" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Non-equilibrium statistical mechanics: from a paradigmatic model to biological transport</a></p><h2 class=""><u>Passive transport</u></h2><p><u>Simple diffusion</u></p><p>small non-polar molecules, like</p><ul><li>Oxygen</li><li>Carbon dioxide</li></ul><p><u>Facilitated diffusion</u></p><p>Large or polar molecules passing through membrane protein channels. Examples of molecules that need protein channels:</p><ul><li>Charged ions</li><li>Glucose</li></ul><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Osmosis">Osmosis</a></u></p><p>Like water through aquaporins</p><h2 class=""><u>Active transport</u></h2><p>Requires energy, for e.g. in the form of ATP.</p><h3 class=""><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Ion%20channel">Ion channel</a>s</u></h3><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion_channel" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ion_channel</a></p><p>Sodium-potassium pump</p><h3 class=""><u>Endocytosis and exocytosis</u></h3><hr><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ptmlvtei8hw" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Cell Transport</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.brightstorm.com/science/biology/cell-functions-and-processes/cell-transport/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://www.brightstorm.com/science/biology/cell-functions-and-processes/cell-transport/</a></p>
