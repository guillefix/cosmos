<p><strong>Soft condensed matter</strong> (often abbreviated to <strong>soft matter</strong>) is basically all forms of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Condensed%20matter%20physics">condensed matter</a> (i.e. many particles more or less bound together (for e.g., by <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Intermolecular%20forces">Intermolecular forces</a>)) that isn't a solid, so that it has features that are easily deformable at low energies (room thermal energies). This includes <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Polymer%20physics">polymers</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Liquid%20crystals">Liquid crystals</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Complex%20fluid%20dynamics">complex fluids</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Granular%20material">Granular material</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Foam">Foam</a>s, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Emulsion">Emulsion</a>s, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Colloid">Colloid</a>s, and many kinds of mixtures, that form mesoscopic structures. Also a lot of stuff in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Biology">life</a> falls under the &quot;soft&quot; category. I like it precisely because of its richness.</p><p>Wiki: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_matter" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_matter</a></p><p><u>Typial features</u></p><ul><li>Mesoscopic lengthscales</li><li>Fluctuations (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Brownian%20motion">Brownian motion</a>)</li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Self-assembly">Self-assembly</a>. <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Block-copolymer%20self-assembly">Block-copolymer self-assembly</a></li></ul><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Statistical%20physics">Statistical physics</a> is important, in particular interplay of energy and entropy, reflected in the free energy.</p><p>For systems of many particles, one uses <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Statistical%20field%20theory">Statistical field theory</a>. Though one can further simplify by ignoring fluctuations, using a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Mean%20field%20theory">Mean field theory</a>.</p><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Soft%20materials">Soft materials</a></u></p><ul><li>Simple liquids (fully homogeneous/isotropic). See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fluid%20mechanics">Fluid mechanics</a>. The fluid may be a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Solution%20(Chemistry)">solution</a>, but that only changes it <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Chemistry">chemical</a> properties, not its physical ones.</li><li>Several types of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Complex%20fluid%20dynamics">complex fluids</a>. Main types:<ul><li>Solid–liquid (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Suspension">Suspension</a>s (particularly <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Colloid">Colloid</a>s) and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Gels">Gels</a>)</li><li>solid–gas (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Granular%20material">Granular material</a>s, and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fibrous%20material">Fibrous material</a>s)</li><li>liquid–gas (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Foam">Foam</a>s)</li><li>liquid–liquid (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Emulsion">Emulsion</a>s)</li></ul></li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Liquid%20crystals">Liquid crystals</a>: nematic, smectic, cholesteric, columnar, etc.</li><li>Several <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Polymer">polymeric</a> materials are soft.
