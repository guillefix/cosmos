Often these are rationally created networks to optimize toward some goal.</p><p><u>Travel time and cost trade-offs</u></p><p>A good example is: airline networks where a compromise between lowering cost (so having more central hubs and spokes to fill planes more fully, than flights between two minor destinations) and length of travel (to satisfy customers) is sought.</p><p>Ferrer i Cancho have one such simple <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007%2F978-3-540-44943-0_7#page-1" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">model</a> to find compromises between mean geodesic distance (travel time) and number of edges (cost).
