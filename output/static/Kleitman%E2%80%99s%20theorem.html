aka “Harris’ Lemma” or “Harris-Kleitman Lemma”

Let A,B⊆P(n)\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(n)A,B⊆P(n) be downsets. Then

∣A∩B∣≥∣A∣∣B∣2n|A \cap B| \geq \frac{|A||B|}{2^n}∣A∩B∣≥​2​n​​​​∣A∣∣B∣​​

A family of sets A⊆P(n)\mathcal{A} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(n)A⊆P(n) is called a downset if A∈AA \in \mathcal{A}A∈A and A⊃BA \supset BA⊃B implies B∈AB \in \mathcal{A}B∈A. Upsets are defined similarly.
