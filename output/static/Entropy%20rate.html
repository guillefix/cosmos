But what if the random variables are dependent? In particular, what if the random variables form a sta- tionary process? We will show, just as in the i.i.d. case, that the entropy H (X 1 , X 2 , . . . , X n ) grows (asymptotically) linearly with n at a rate H ( X ), which we will call the entropy rate of the process. The interpretation of H ( X ) as the best achievable data compression will await the analysis in Chapter 5 (from Cover&amp;Thomas book).
