<p>A <strong>phoretic mechanism</strong> of colloids, is any mechanism/effect that causes <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Colloid">colloidal particles</a> to move in a way that is partially deterministic (unlike <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Diffusion">Diffusion</a>), due to the gradient of some physical quantity (<sub>This seems to be the working definition, judging from what I've read</sub>). These may also called <strong>transport mechanisms</strong>.</p><p>These are important in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Active%20matter">Active matter</a>, in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Biophysics">Biophysics</a>, and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Nanotechnology">Nanotechnology</a>. In particular phoretic effects can make a colloidal particle self-propelling.</p><p>A large class of mechanism for colloid transport are due to <strong>interfacial forces</strong>, due to non-trivial <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Microhydrodynamics">Microhydrodynamics</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Chemical%20reaction">Chemical reaction</a>s, or other effects.</p><p><big><i aria-hidden="true" class="fa fa-star-o"></i> See <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.annualreviews.org/doi/abs/10.1146/annurev.fl.21.010189.000425" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Colloid Transport by Interfacial Forces</a></big>. See also the more recent <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://hal.in2p3.fr/tel-00597477/document" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Manipulation of Colloids by Osmotic Forces </a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://link.springer.com/article/10.1140/epje/i2008-10446-8" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Generic theory of colloidal transport</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0034-4885/73/12/126601/meta" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Thermal non-equilibrium transport in colloids</a></p><h2 class=""><u>Phoretic mechanisms</u></h2><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Diffusiophoresis">Diffusiophoresis</a></u></p><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Osmiophoresis">Osmiophoresis</a></u></p><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Electrophoresis">Electrophoresis</a></u></p><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Thermophoresis">Thermophoresis</a></u></p><p><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phoretic%20mechanisms%20of%20self-propelled%20colloids">Phoretic mechanisms of self-propelled colloids</a></u></p>
