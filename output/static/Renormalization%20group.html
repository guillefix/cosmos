The general framework (as applied to critical phenomena) is presented below. For other applications, the later steps will be different, but the general setup is the same.</p><p>1. Define <strong>RG scheme</strong> (often involving <strong>coarse graining</strong> and <strong>scaling</strong>; this is the case, for instance, in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Real-space%20renormalization%20group">Real-space renormalization group</a>, see <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#RG%20scheme">RG scheme</a>), that defines new variables, while leaving the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Partition%20function">Partition function</a> fixed (or at least approximately fixed). Note that even though the partition function is fixed (and so all macroscopic variables are too), the microscopic Boltzmann distribution function need not stay fixed as a consequence (see <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://arxiv.org/abs/1608.08225" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">this paper</a>, see reply to the article actually).</p><p>2. This scheme produces an RG transformation on the couplings/parameters of the theory. This transformation, if iterated, produces an <strong>RG flow</strong> in the space of parameters. The flow can indeed be analyzed with the tools of the theory of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Dynamical%20system">Dynamical system</a>s</p><p>3. Any point near a fixed point in the space of parameters has <strong>relevant and irrelevant</strong> (and possibly marginal) <strong>directions</strong>. These correspond to natural coordinates related to the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Invariant%20manifolds%20in%20dynamical%20systems">unstable and stable manifolds</a> of a fixed point, which in the linear neighbourhood of the fixed point are called <strong>scaling variables</strong>. Relevant directions are the ones that determine the long-time dynamics under the RG flow.</p><p>4. Changes in tunable parameters of the theory (like temperature, volume, external magnetic field, etc.) can be related to changes in coupling constants that produce the same change in the free energy. These changes should be along relevant directions because tunable parameters can affect the qualitative macroscopic behaviour of the theory, and so should affect the long-time behaviour of the theory under the RG flow.</p><p>5. A <strong>critical surface</strong> corresponds to the stable manifold of a saddle fixed point (this manifold is also called separatrix in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Dynamical%20system">Dynamical system</a>s theory, because it separates qualitatively different future flows, corresponding to different phases, in a physical system). A critical point of a family of theories parametrized by a parameter, and spanning a 1D manifold (curve) in the whole space of theories is the intersection of this curve with the critical surface.</p><p>6. Theories near the critical point evolve to the vicinity of the fixed point under a finite number of iterations of the RG transformation. Theories with slightly different tuning parameters evolve to slightly different points in the vicinity of the critical point.
