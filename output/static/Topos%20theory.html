<p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topos" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Topos</a> is a category that behaves like the category of sheaves of sets on a topological space (or more generally: on a site). Topoi behave much like the category of sets and possess a notion of localization; they are in a sense a generalization of point-set topology.[1] The Grothendieck topoi find applications in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Algebraic%20geometry">Algebraic geometry</a>; the more general elementary topoi are used in logic.</p><p>A topos is a category with:</p><p>A) finite limits and colimits,</p><p>B) exponentials,</p><p>C) a subobject classifier.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://math.ucr.edu/home/baez/topos.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Topos Theory in a Nutshell</a></p><p><u>Higher topos theory</u></p>
