</div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p><em>How things move</em></p><p>A space (in the mathematical sense, for a continuous space, one often uses a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Manifold.html">Manifold</a>, or a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Topological%2520space.html">Topological space</a>), with a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Function.html">Function</a> (a.k.a. a map) that describes how a point in the space evolves (in &quot;time&quot;).</p><h2 class=""><u>Types of dynamical systems</u></h2><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Measure-theoretical%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Measure-theoretical dynamical system</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Topological%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Topological dynamical system</a></p><p><strong><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Continuous%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Continuous dynamical system</a></strong> are dynamical systems where the space is continuous. It is often represented as a system of 1st order O.D.Es. Linear dynamical systems (O.D.E.s linear) are easy to analyze, and can be analyzed by looking at the eigenvalues of the Jacobian.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Discrete%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Discrete dynamical system</a> are those where the space is discrete. They are often represented as systems of difference equations (see <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Nonlinear%2520maps.html">Nonlinear maps</a>).</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Measure-theoretical%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Measure-theoretical dynamical system</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Topological%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Topological dynamical system</a></p><p>The richest class of dynamical systems are <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Nonlinear%2520system.html">Nonlinear system</a>s</p><p>A dynamical system, whether continuous or discrete, can be partitioned (<strong>coarse-grained</strong>), so that its dynamics can be studied as <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Symbolic%2520dynamics.html">Symbolic dynamics</a>. If the system is a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Probabilistic%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Probabilistic dynamical system</a>, then the coarse-graining gives rise to a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Stochastic%2520processes.html">stochastic process</a></p><h2 class=""><u>Deterministic vs probabilistic dynamics</u></h2><p>Dynamical systems generally describe deterministic processes. Probabilistic processes are described as <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Stochastic%2520processes.html">Stochastic processes</a>. However, these can sometimes be described as deterministic dynamics of probability distributions, or as a probability measure over a deterministic process (i.e. a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Probabilistic%2520dynamical%2520system.html">Probabilistic dynamical system</a>).</p><hr><p>Dynamical system software: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://dsweb.siam.org/Software" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://dsweb.siam.org/Software</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamical_system_(definition)#Geometrical_cases" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">See Wiki page for good intro and different kinds</a></p><h3 class=""><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.scholarpedia.org/article/Encyclopedia:Dynamical_systems" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Encyclopedia:Dynamical systems</a></h3><p>Dynamical systems on complex space (particularly <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Discrete%2520dynamical%2520systems.html">discrete</a> ones): <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Complex%2520dynamics.html">Complex dynamics</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://chaosbook.org/course1/Syllabus.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Nonlinear Dynamics 1: Geometry of Chaos by Predrag CvitanoviÄ‡ (ChaosBook course)</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floquet_theory" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floquet_theory</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.math.lsa.umich.edu/~rauch/256/turingexample.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Turing instability</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=csInNn6pfT4&amp;t=2s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Fixed Points</a></p><hr></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
