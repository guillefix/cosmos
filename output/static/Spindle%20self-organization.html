They do this because they consider the simplest theory that is consistent with all the data.</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Supporting%2520infomation%2520(annotated).html">Supporting infomation (annotated)</a></p><p>Theory based on that developed in this paper: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.80.011917" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Fluctuating hydrodynamics and microrheology of a dilute suspension of swimming bacteria</a>. Some parts can be derived using <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://journals.aps.org/pre/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevE.67.061709" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Poisson-bracket approach to the dynamics of nematic liquid crystals</a>. </p><p>How changes in volume due to microtubule polymerization (gaining the dimers) can also add to active stress, as in the case of cells growing in tissues: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.pnas.org/content/107/49/20863.abstract" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Fluidization of tissues by cell division and apoptosis</a></p><h2 class=""><u>Experimental validation</u></h2><p><u>Materials and apparatus</u></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://openpolscope.org/docs/OldenbourgLC-PolScopeLabManual2005.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">LC-PolScope</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://openpolscope.org/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://openpolscope.org/</a>. In particular, they measure correlations of the fluctuations at each pixel in the image relative to the time-average value of that pixel.
