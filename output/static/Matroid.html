<p><em>Matroid theory</em></p><p>A <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matroid" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">matroid</a> is a structure that captures and generalizes the notion of linear independence in vector spaces. There are many equivalent ways to define a matroid, the most significant being in terms of independent sets, bases, circuits, closed sets or flats, closure operators, and rank functions.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4EvpzV_3RXI" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Matroids as a Theory of Independence by Federico Ardila</a></p><p>See books on matroid theory. matroid theory lawler</p><p>They give a theoretical foundation for <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Greedy%20algorithm">Greedy algorithm</a>s apparently</p>
