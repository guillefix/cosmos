</a> 4th November 2016 at 9:43am </div> </div> <div class=" tc-reveal"> <div class="tc-tags-wrapper"></div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p><strong>Microtubules</strong> (micro- + tube + -ule) are a component of the cytoskeleton, found throughout the cytoplasm. </p><p>These tubular polymers of tubulin can grow as long as 50 micrometres and are highly dynamic. The outer diameter of a microtubule is about 24 nm while the inner diameter is about 12 nm. They are found in eukaryotic cells, as well as some bacteria, and are formed by the polymerization of a dimer of two globular proteins, alpha and beta tubulin.</p><p>(<a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microtubule" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microtubule</a>)</p><p>Some times of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Molecular%2520motors.html">Molecular motors</a> walk along microtubules to transport molecular cargo inside a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Cell%2520(Biology).html">cell</a>.</p><p>They are the primary component of the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Spindle%2520(Cell%2520biology).html">Spindle</a> which separates chromosomes during cellular division.</p><p><u>Microtubule turnover</u>: the process by which microtubules decay, and are replaced. &quot;Turnover&quot; can also refer to the <em>rate</em> of this process. See <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.thefreedictionary.com/turn+over" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">turn over (definition)</a>: To be replaced by something else of the same kind.
