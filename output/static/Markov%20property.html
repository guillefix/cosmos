<p>A <strong>Markov property</strong> refers to any of a certain class of independence properties of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Random%20variable">Random variable</a>s defined on the nodes of a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Network">Network</a>.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Pairwise%20Markov%20property">Pairwise Markov property</a>: Any two non-adjacent variables are conditionally independent given all other variables</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Local%20Markov%20property">Local Markov property</a>: A variable is conditionally independent of all other variables given its neighbors (<a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lekCh_i32iE&amp;list=PL6Xpj9I5qXYEcOhn7TqghAJ6NAPrNmUBH&amp;index=37#t=14m37s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">video</a>).</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Global%20Markov%20property">Global Markov property</a>: Any two subsets of variables are conditionally independent given a separating subset</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Markov_random_field#/Definition" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">here</a>, and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Markov%20network">Markov network</a></p><hr><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Stochastic%20process">Stochastic process</a>es that satisfy the Markov property are known as <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Markov%20process">Markov process</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lfHX2hHRMVQ&amp;list=PL7-jPKtc4r78-wCZcQn5IqyuWhBZ8fOxT&amp;index=2&amp;spfreload=1#t=3m25s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">vid</a></p>
