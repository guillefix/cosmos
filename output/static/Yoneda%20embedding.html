<p>A <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Functor">Functor</a>, from a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Category">Category</a> to {the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Functor%20category">Category of functors</a> from the category to Set} where objects are mapped to to their corresponding <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Representable%20functor">Representable functor</a>s, and morphisms are <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Natural%20transformation">Natural transformation</a>s induced by morphisms in the original category. The fact that these are natural transformations turns out to be equivalent to the associativity axiom of the categeory.</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eaJmUUogb6g" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">here</a></p>
