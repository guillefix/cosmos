alternative hypothesis to the frequentist significance level.</p><ul><li>I am thinking about two discrete hypotheses, and two possible values of the data for now, and with some mild You accept a null hypothesis rejection with p=5% unless you think the alternative had a less than 5% chance of being true. I.e. you find the alternative less likely than chance, given than chance had a probability of p=5%</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://stats.stackexchange.com/questions/2272/whats-the-difference-between-a-confidence-interval-and-a-credible-interval" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://stats.stackexchange.com/questions/2272/whats-the-difference-between-a-confidence-interval-and-a-credible-interval</a></p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
