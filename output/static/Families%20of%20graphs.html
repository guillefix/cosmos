A <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Directed%20acyclic%20craph">Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)</a> is a well known sub-type. </p><p><span id="hypergraph"><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Hypergraphs">Hypergraphs</a></span> are sets of elements with <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relation_%28mathematics%29" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">relations</a> that include more than a pair of elements (i.e. they are members of a higher <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_product" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">cartesian product</a>).</p><p>Hypergraphs can equivalently be represented as <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Bipartite%20Networks">Bipartite Networks</a>, where there are two types of edges (a special case of a <em>multipartite network</em>, where there are many types). On the other hand, a <em>multiplex network</em> is one that has multiple types of edges.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Tree%20(Graph%20Theory)">Trees</a> are connected (<small>can reach all vertices following edges</small>), undirected networks that contain no closed loops. A <em>forest</em> is a disconnected graph whose connected parts are trees.</p><p>A <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Planar%20network">Planar network</a> is a network that can be drawn on a plane without having any edges cross. It is a special case of a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Spatial%20network">Spatial network</a>.</p><p><em>Temporal networks</em> are those for which the set of edges and/or nodes varies with a time parameter.</p><p>A <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Similarity%20network">Similarity network</a> is one that expressed how similar entities (expressed as the nodes) are. The degree of similarity being the weight of the node.</p>
