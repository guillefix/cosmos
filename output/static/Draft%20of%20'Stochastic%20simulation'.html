<p>Simulation of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Stochastic%20process">Stochastic process</a>es, often based on <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Master%20equation">Master equation</a>s</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Gillespie's%20algorithm">Gillespie's algorithm</a>. Calculate next time for a reaction to occur (from <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Exponential%20distribution">Exponential distribution</a> with rate = sum of rates of all reactions), and then decide which of the possible reactions occurs according to their probabilities, which are proportional to their <strong>propensity functions</strong>, which are basically reaction rates (the probability of a particular reaction occuring on an infinitesimal time interval, which depends on the state of the system, often a set of species concentrations). </p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Tau-leap%20method">Tau-leap method</a>. Assume propensity functions don't change.
