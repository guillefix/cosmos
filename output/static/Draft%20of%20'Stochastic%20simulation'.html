Calculate next time for a reaction to occur (from <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Exponential%2520distribution.html">Exponential distribution</a> with rate = sum of rates of all reactions), and then decide which of the possible reactions occurs according to their probabilities, which are proportional to their <strong>propensity functions</strong>, which are basically reaction rates (the probability of a particular reaction occuring on an infinitesimal time interval, which depends on the state of the system, often a set of species concentrations). </p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Tau-leap%2520method.html">Tau-leap method</a>. Assume propensity functions don't change.
