<p>Diffusiophoresis is the process by which particles move trough a chemical concentration gradient, due to an attractive or repulsive interaction between the particle and the chemical compound. It is a kind of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phoretic%20mechanisms%20of%20colloids">phoretic mechanism of colloids</a>.</p><p><img src="http://www.sas.upenn.edu/~tidema/Images/diffusiophoresis.png" width="300"></p><p>Essentially, the surface of the particle, due to <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Intermolecular%20forces">Intermolecular forces</a> (or other entropic forces), can be attracted, or repelled to the solvent molecules. If there is a gradient in the concentration of these, they can exert a net force on the particle causing it to gain a certain velocity (and the particle will exert a force on the fluid, causing it to slip over its surface).</p><p>In <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Self-diffusiophoresis">Self-diffusiophoresis</a> (a kind of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Self-propelled%20particle">self-propulsion</a>), the particle itself produces the compound it interacts with.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/la00050a035" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/la00050a035</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external"
