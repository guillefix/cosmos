Some features that are important in the behaviour of an evolving system.

Important features:

  • Evolvability (wiki page). May be defined as "the capacity of a system for adaptive evolution"; however, this is often called adaptability (I think). Evolvability is then defined just as "the number of other phenotypes potentially accessible from a phenotype" (see this paper or Robustness and evolvability: a paradox resolved)
  • Modularity. See Self-assembly, modularity, and physical complexity, and complexity. Homologues, Natural Kinds and the Evolution of Modularity
  • Robustness. See Mutational robustness. Environmental robustness. There are genotypic and phenotypic robustness. See Common features of GP maps
