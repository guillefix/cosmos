<p>A <strong>mode of inheritance</strong> refers to a particular way a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phenotype">Phenotype</a> may be inherited.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Sex%20linkage">Sex-linked</a>: the gene is in a sex chromosome (X or Y)</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Autosomal%20inheritance">Autosomal</a>: the gene is in a non-sex chromosome (an <em>autosome</em>).</p><p>Dominant, recessive, etc: <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Genetic%20dominance">Genetic dominance</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VQmZXw8UBh4" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Pedigree analysis</a></p>
