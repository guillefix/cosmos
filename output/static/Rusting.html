<p>Rusting is an <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Redox%20reaction">oxidation reaction</a>, that occurs when some <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Metal">Metal</a>s, like <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Iron">Iron</a> are in contact with <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Water">Water</a> and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Oxygen">Oxygen</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/education/guides/zqjsgk7/revision/5" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://www.bbc.co.uk/education/guides/zqjsgk7/revision/5</a></p><p><img src="http://a.files.bbci.co.uk/bam/live/content/z46gd2p/large"></p><p>Aluminium does not rust (corrode) because its surface is protected by a natural layer of aluminium oxide. This prevents the metal below from coming into contact with air (containing oxygen).</p><p>Unlike rust, which can flake off the surface of iron and steel objects, the layer of aluminium oxide does not flake off.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/education/guides/zqjsgk7/revision/6" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Preventing rusting</a></p>
