Mittag-Leffer's theorem

cosmos 31st October 2018 at 6:15pm

https://blogs.scientificamerican.com/roots-of-unity/nalini-joshis-favorite-theorem/

A theorem that says that you can describe a Meromorphic function with only information about where the poles are, and their degree.

It is one of the most powerful ways to describe Transcendental functions, and it is applied in many areas including Random matrix theory. There are now connections, through something called "doubly periodic functions", with curves and Algebraic geometry
