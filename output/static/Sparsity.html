<p>The property of there being much fewer things than there could be. Formally, given a collection of sets of different cardinalities, if the smallest ones are much smaller than the largest ones, then they are called <em>sparse</em>. <sub>&quot;smaller&quot;/&quot;larger&quot; referring to cardinality</sub></p><p>In <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Matrix">Matrix</a> theory, a <b>sparse matrix</b> often refers to one with few non-zero entries.</p><p>In <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Signal%20processing">Signal processing</a> a <b>sparse signal</b> often refers to one which has few non-zero <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Fourier%20coefficient">Fourier components</a></p>
