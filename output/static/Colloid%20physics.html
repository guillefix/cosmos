<p>Branch of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Physics">Physics</a> dealing with physical properties of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Colloid">colloidal systems</a> (i.e. motion, forces, etc. at the scale of the colloidal system).</p><p>The branch of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Soft%20matter%20physics">soft matter</a> dealing with colloids has a close connection with the other subjects of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Condensed%20matter%20physics">Condensed matter physics</a>, like <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Solid-state%20physics">Solid-state physics</a>. This is because colloidal <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Suspension">Suspension</a>s in many ways can behave analogously to solids, whether crystalline or glassy. Colloidal particles have also been used as model systems for atoms or molecules, and so there are some connections with <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Atomic%20physics">Atomic physics</a> and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Molecular%20physics">Molecular physics</a>.</p><h3 class=""><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Microhydrodynamics">Microhydrodynamics</a> of colloids</u></h3><ul><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Low%20Reynolds%20number">Low Reynolds number</a></li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Hydrodynamic%20interaction">Hydrodynamic interaction</a></li></ul><h3 class=""><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phoretic%20mechanisms%20of%20colloids">Phoretic mechanisms of colloids</a></u></h3><p>These are important in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Active%20matter">Active matter</a> (see <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Active%20colloid">Active colloid</a>), in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Biophysics">Biophysics</a>, and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Nanotechnology">Nanotechnology</a>.</p>
