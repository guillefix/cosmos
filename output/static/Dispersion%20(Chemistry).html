tc-reveal"><p>A <strong>dispersion</strong> is a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Materials%2520science.html">material</a> comprising more than one phase where at least one of the phases consists of finely divided phase domains, often in the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Colloid.html">colloidal</a> size range, dispersed throughout a continuous phase.</p><p>A <u>continuous phase</u> is a phase not interrupted in space.</p><p>A <u>dispersed phase</u> is a phase constituted of particles of any size and of any nature dispersed in a continuous phase of a different composition.</p><p>The <u>dispersion medium</u> is the matrix for the dispersed phase. The dispersion medium is the continuous phase of the dispersion.</p><p>Source from IUPAC: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://espace.library.uq.edu.au/view/UQ:266979/UQ266979_OA.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Terminology of polymers and polymerization processes in dispersed systems (IUPAC Recommendations 2011)*</a>. </p><p>Depending on the size of the particles in the dispersed phase we have:</p><ul><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Solution%2520(Chemistry).html">Solution</a>, for size less than a nanometer.</li><li><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Colloid.html">Colloid</a>, for size between a nanometer and a micrometer.</li><li>Coarse dispersion, for a size larger than a micrometer.</li></ul><p>&quot;Dispersion&quot;, without adjective, is often used to refer to the colloidal regime.</p><h2 class=""><u>Dispersion types</u></h2><p>For phases with particles of colloidal size or larger.
