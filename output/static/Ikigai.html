<p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ikigai" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ikigai</a></p><p>Everyone, according to Japanese culture, has an ikigai. Finding it requires a deep and often lengthy search of self. Such a search is important to the cultural belief that discovering one's ikigai brings satisfaction and meaning to life.</p><p>In the culture of Okinawa, ikigai is thought of as &quot;a reason to get up in the morning&quot;; that is, a reason to enjoy life. In a TED Talk, Dan Buettner suggested ikigai as one of the reasons people in the area had such long lives</p>
