<p>A <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Separation%20process">Separation process</a> to separate <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Solid">Solid</a>s from <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fluid">Fluid</a>s in a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Mixture">Mixture</a> (liquids or gases) by adding a medium through which only the fluid can pass.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Filtration" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Filtration</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/education/guides/zgbqtfr/revision/8" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://www.bbc.co.uk/education/guides/zgbqtfr/revision/8</a></p><p><img src="http://a.files.bbci.co.uk/bam/live/content/z6sk2hv/large"></p><hr><p>When a product is made as a solution, one way to separate it from the solvent is to make crystals.
