A relation is a subset of a Cartesian product.

A relation is often used to refer to a binary relation, which is a subset of X×YX \times YX×Y. An element x∈Xx \in Xx∈X is said to be related to y∈Yy \in Yy∈Y (denoted xRyxRyxRy) if the pair (x,y)∈R⊂X×X(x,y) \in R \subset X \times X(x,y)∈R⊂X×X.

A relation on XXX is used to refer to a subset of X×XX \times XX×X.

A Function F:X→YF: X \rightarrow YF:X→Y defines a relation, but not all relations correspond to functions.
