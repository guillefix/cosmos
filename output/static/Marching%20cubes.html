<p>Marching cubes is a simple algorithm for creating a triangle mesh from an implicit function (one of the form f(x, y, z) = 0). It works by iterating (&quot;marching&quot;) over a uniform grid of cubes superimposed over a region of the function.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://graphics.stanford.edu/~mdfisher/MarchingCubes.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://graphics.stanford.edu/~mdfisher/MarchingCubes.html</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LfttaAepYJ8" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LfttaAepYJ8</a></p><p>The triangles match up because adjacent cubes share faces, and the edges of triangles on each face are only determined by the pattern in the face!</p>
