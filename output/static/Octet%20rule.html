<p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Octet_rule" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Octet_rule</a></p><p>The octet rule is a chemical rule of thumb that reflects observation that atoms of main-group <strong>elements tend to combine in such a way that each atom has eight electrons in its valence shell</strong>, giving it the same electronic configuration as a noble gas. The rule is especially applicable to carbon, nitrogen, oxygen, and the halogens, but also to metals such as sodium or magnesium.</p>
