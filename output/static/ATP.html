<p><strong>Adenosine triphosphate</strong></p><p>Molecule that stores chemical energy for the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Cell">Cell</a>. Produced by <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Cellular%20respiration">Cellular respiration</a></p><p>Adenine + Ribose + 3 <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phosphate">Phosphate</a> groups. These are negative, and the fact that these three negatively charged groups are held together explains why the molecule holds so much (electric) energy.</p><p>ATP hydrolyses to ADP (adenosine diphosphate) and phosphate, by unbonding one of the phosphate groups, and releasing energy.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KJG6HA5xSxY" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Video</a></p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Metabolic%20mechanisms">Metabolic mechanisms</a> to see how ATP can make unfarovable reactions happen!</p><h2 class=""><u>ATP to ADP</u></h2><p><u>Energetics</u></p><ul><li>Separate negative charges</li><li>Increase electron delocalization (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Resonance%20structure">Resonance structure</a>s)</li><li>More possible interactions with water of the released <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phosphate">Phosphate</a></li></ul><p>High activation energy. needs <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Enzyme">Enzyme</a></p><hr><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Adenosine_triphosphate" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Adenosine_triphosphate</a></p>
