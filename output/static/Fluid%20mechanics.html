</div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p>The branch of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Mechanics.html">Mechanics</a> that deals with the motion and the forces that affect <strong>fluids</strong></p><p>A <strong>fluid</strong> is a piece of matter that has no, or negligible, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Viscosity%2520and%2520elasticity.html">elasticity</a>. This means it flows under virtually any applied force.</p><p>There are three main <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Phase%2520transition.html">phases</a> of matter that are fluid:</p><ul><li><strong>Liquid</strong>. Fully homogeneous/isotropic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Condensed%2520matter%2520physics.html">condensed</a> phase composed of atoms.</li><li><strong>Gas</strong>. Fully homogeneous/isotropic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Condensed%2520matter%2520physics.html">non-condensed</a> phase composed of atoms.</li><li><strong>Plasma</strong>. Fully homogeneous/isotropic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Condensed%2520matter%2520physics.html">non-condensed</a> phase composed of ionized atoms and/or other charged particles. See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Plasma%2520physics.html">Plasma physics</a>.</li></ul><p>More complex fluid phases, often composed of mixtures are called <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Complex%2520fluid%2520dynamics.html">complex fluids</a>.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Fluid%2520dynamics.html">Fluid dynamics</a> describes the causes of motion, i.e. the forces and torques that can affect fluids, and how these affect their motion. <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Magnetohydrodynamics.html">Magnetohydrodynamics</a> and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="Electrohydrodynamics.html">Electrohydrodynamics</a> describe the dynamics of an electrically conductive fluid.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Fluid%2520kinematics.html">Fluid kinematics</a> (just like kinematics, in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Mechanics.html">Mechanics</a>) describes the possible motion of fluids.</p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
