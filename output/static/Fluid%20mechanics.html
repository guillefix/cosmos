<p>The branch of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Mechanics">Mechanics</a> that deals with the motion and the forces that affect <strong>fluids</strong></p><p>A <strong>fluid</strong> is a piece of matter that has no, or negligible, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Viscosity%20and%20elasticity">elasticity</a>. This means it flows under virtually any applied force.</p><p>There are three main <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Phase%20transition">phases</a> of matter that are fluid:</p><ul><li><strong>Liquid</strong>. Fully homogeneous/isotropic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Condensed%20matter%20physics">condensed</a> phase composed of atoms.</li><li><strong>Gas</strong>. Fully homogeneous/isotropic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Condensed%20matter%20physics">non-condensed</a> phase composed of atoms.</li><li><strong>Plasma</strong>. Fully homogeneous/isotropic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Condensed%20matter%20physics">non-condensed</a> phase composed of ionized atoms and/or other charged particles. See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Plasma%20physics">Plasma physics</a>.</li></ul><p>More complex fluid phases, often composed of mixtures are called <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Complex%20fluid%20dynamics">complex fluids</a>.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fluid%20dynamics">Fluid dynamics</a> describes the causes of motion, i.e. the forces and torques that can affect fluids, and how these affect their motion. <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Magnetohydrodynamics">Magnetohydrodynamics</a> and <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Electrohydrodynamics">Electrohydrodynamics</a> describe the dynamics of an electrically conductive fluid.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fluid%20kinematics">Fluid kinematics</a> (just like kinematics, in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Mechanics">Mechanics</a>) describes the possible motion of fluids.</p>
