occur at the interface of two phases, including solid–liquid interfaces, solid–gas interfaces, solid–vacuum interfaces, and liquid–gas interfaces. It includes the fields of surface chemistry and surface physics.</p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Materials%2520science.html">Materials science</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Condensed%2520matter%2520physics.html">Condensed matter physics</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Chemistry.html">Chemistry</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_science" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface_science</a></p><h2 class=""><u>Surface chemistry</u></h2><h2 class=""><u>Surface physics</u></h2><h3 class=""><u>Fluid dynamics at interfaces</u></h3><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://www.annualreviews.org/doi/abs/10.1146/annurev.fl.21.010189.000425" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Colloid Transport by Interfacial Forces</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Interfacial%2520forces.html">Interfacial forces</a></p><p><u>Fluid/fluid interfaces</u></p><p>Governed mostly by (apparent) discontinuities in stress, particularly surface tension. These are known as &quot;Marangoni effects&quot;, or &quot;capillary-driven flow&quot;.</p><p><u>Solid/fluid interfaces</u></p><p>Governed mostly by slip velocity at the interface.</p><p>These are responsible for several of the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Phoretic%2520mechanisms%2520of%2520colloids.html">Phoretic mechanisms of colloids</a>, which cause them to move along gradients of some quantity.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Surface%2520tension.html">Surface tension</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Pervaporation.html">Pervaporation</a></p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
