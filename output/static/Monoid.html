<p>In abstract algebra, a branch of mathematics, a monoid is an algebraic structure with a single associative binary operation and an identity element. They are a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Semigroup">Semigroup</a> with identity.</p><p>They are also a one-object <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Category">Category</a>, with arrows corresponding to elements in the monoid, and composition corresponding to monoid multiplication. <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aZjhqkD6k6w&amp;index=5&amp;list=PLbgaMIhjbmEnaH_LTkxLI7FMa2HsnawM_#t=32m50s" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Video</a></p><p>There is also the category of all monoids, with arrows corresponding to <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Monoid%20homomorphism">Monoid homomorphism</a> (defined in the natural way).</p><p>It can also be constructed as an <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Algebra%20over%20a%20monad">Algebra over a monad</a></p><h3 class=""><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Free%20monoid">Free monoid</a></h3>
