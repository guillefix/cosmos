can distinguish two types of important nodes in directed networks. We describe them for the case of an information network, like WWW first:</p><ul><li><em>authorities</em> are nodes that contain useful information on a topic of interest</li><li><em>hubs</em> are nodes that point us to the best authorities</li></ul><p>This idea was implemented by Kleinberg into the <em>hyperlink-induced topic search</em> or <em>HITS</em> algorithm. The mathematical definitions that tries to capture the above intuition are:</p><ul><li><em>authority centrality</em>: vertex pointed by many hubs (i.e. by many nodes with high hub
