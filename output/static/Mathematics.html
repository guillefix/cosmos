</span> </div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p>Mathematics is the study of <strong>structures</strong> themselves. These are necessary in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Science.html">Science</a> and in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Art.html">Art</a>, as both require the invention of structures to either explain (and thus <em>understand</em>) the world, or for any other purpose (in the case of Art).</p><p>Mathematics, however, doesn't concern itself with the purposes or details of particular structures; rather, it commits itself with the abstract properties common among many structures. </p><p>Math is just what happens when one decides to think logically. It is the study of the logical consequences of arbitrary axioms.</p><p>It is both the Art and Science of the <strong>structure of structures</strong>. It studies many structures in the world, and creates an abstract structure to understand them. In this sense it is a Science. It also creates new unobserved abstract structures, often, generalizing observed ones. In this sense it is an Art.</p><h3 class=""><u>Common structures</u></h3><ul><li>One can have things and sets of things (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Set%2520theory.html">Set theory</a>)</li><li>Relations among things<ul><li>One can get new things by putting together old things, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Abstract%2520algebra.html">Abstract algebra</a></li><li>or things may just have some given relations (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Graph%2520theory.html">Graph theory</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Network%2520theory.html">Network theory</a>)</li></ul></li><li>One can count things (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Number%2520theory.html">Number theory</a>)</li><li>One can have many, many things (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Analysis.html">Analysis</a>). For example, it seems like there is an <em>infinity</em> of instants in every second..<ul><li>Can we define things that make sense, as the number of things keeps growing? <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Limits%2520and%2520infinity.html">Limits and infinity</a><ul><li>Can we still add and subtract these things? If yes, -&gt; <em>Differential stuff</em>! (e.g. <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Differential%2520equations.html">Differential equations</a>, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Differential%2520geometry.html">Differential geometry</a>)</li></ul></li></ul></li><li>Things may change, for example, in time<ul><li>Things may undergo processes, that can be composed, one after the other. <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Category%2520theory.html">Category theory</a></li><li>Things may evolve according to some given rule: if you're here, go there. <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Dynamical%2520system.html">Dynamical system</a></li></ul></li><li>Things can have shape (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Geometry.html">Geometry</a>)<ul><li>Things can have a shape, made out of many, many flat pieces. If done right, we get <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Differential%2520geometry.html">Differential geometry</a></li><li>Can we get shapes by combining things in certain ways? <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Algebraic%2520geometry.html">Algebraic geometry</a></li><li>Some shapes are more similar than others (<a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Topology.html">Topology</a>)<ul><li>Can we compare shapes by subdividing a shape in many (possibly many <big><em>many</em></big>) parts, and looking at how these parts relate and overlap? <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Point-set%2520topology.html">Point-set topology</a></li><li>Can one combine shapes? <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Algebraic%2520topology.html">Algebraic topology</a></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul><p><u>Maps of mathematics</u></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external"
