<p>In <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Information%20theory">Information theory</a>, and in particular, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Data%20transmission">Data transmission</a>, a <strong>finite state channel</strong> (FSC) is a discrete-time channel where the distribution of the channel output depends on both the channel input and the underlying channel state. This allows the channel output to depend implicitly on previous inputs and outputs via the channel state. </p><p>The channel can be modelled as a stochastic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Finite-state%20transducer">Finite-state transducer</a>.</p><p><u>See here for more</u>: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://pfister.ee.duke.edu/thesis/chap4.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://pfister.ee.duke.edu/thesis/chap4.pdf</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://web.stanford.edu/~glynn/papers/2002/HollidayGoldsmithG02a.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Entropy and Mutual Information for Markov Channels with General Inputs</a></p><p>Blackwell, Breiman, and Thomasian introduced indecomposable FSC (IFSC) in [7] and proved the natural analogue of the channel coding theorem for them.
