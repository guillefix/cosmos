</span> <span class="tc-drop-down tc-reveal" hidden="true"></span> </span> </div> </div> <div class="tc-tiddler-body tc-reveal"><p>In <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Information%2520theory.html">Information theory</a>, and in particular, <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Data%2520transmission.html">Data transmission</a>, a <strong>finite state channel</strong> (FSC) is a discrete-time channel where the distribution of the channel output depends on both the channel input and the underlying channel state. This allows the channel output to depend implicitly on previous inputs and outputs via the channel state. </p><p>The channel can be modelled as a stochastic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Finite-state%2520transducer.html">Finite-state transducer</a>.</p><p><u>See here for more</u>: <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://pfister.ee.duke.edu/thesis/chap4.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">http://pfister.ee.duke.edu/thesis/chap4.pdf</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://web.stanford.edu/~glynn/papers/2002/HollidayGoldsmithG02a.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Entropy and Mutual Information for Markov Channels with General Inputs</a></p><p>Blackwell, Breiman, and Thomasian introduced indecomposable FSC (IFSC) in [7] and proved the natural analogue of the channel coding theorem for them. Birch discusses the achievable information rates of IFSCs in [5], and computes bounds for a few simple examples. </p><h3 class=""><u>Examples of finite state channels</u></h3><ul><li>Discrete-time <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Linear%2520filter.html">Linear filter</a> channels with AWGN</li><li>Dicode erasure channel</li><li>Finite state Z-channel</li></ul><h3 class=""><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Trellis%2520diagram.html">Trellis diagram</a>s</u></h3><p>Similar to the mapping between Boolean lattices and directed percolation. See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Relations%2520between%2520the%2520stability%2520of%2520Boolean%2520networks%2520and%2520percolation.html">Relations between the stability of Boolean networks and percolation</a>.</p><h3 class=""><u>Definitions and basic properties of FSCs</u></h3><p><img src="http://i.imgur.com/kUKuIuz.png" width="500"></p><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Markov%2520chain.html">Markov chain</a></p><p>In the thesis he considers a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Markov%2520input%2520process.html">Markov input process</a> as <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Information%2520source.html">Information source</a></p><p>Combining the Markov Input Process and the Finite State Channel, gives a new Markov process over the states given by the cross product of the states of the channel and of the input. They label this new set of states by integers too. This combined process is what they call a <b>Finite state process</b> (FSP).</p><h3 class=""><u><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Entropy%2520rate%2520of%2520a%2520finite%2520state%2520process.html">Entropy rate of a finite state process</a></u></h3><p>See <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Random%2520matrix%2520product.html">Random matrix product</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://ieeexplore.ieee.org/xpl/login.jsp?tp=&amp;arnumber=1228304&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fieeexplore.ieee.org%2Fiel5%2F8703%2F27553%2F01228304.pdf%3Farnumber%3D1228304" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Capacity of finite state markov channels with general inputs</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://arxiv.org/pdf/1303.3656v1.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">A Randomized Approach to the Capacity of Finite-State Channels</a></p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="http://cs-www.cs.yale.edu/homes/yry/readings/wireless/wireless_readings/fsmc.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Capacity, mutual information, and coding for finite-state Markov channels</a></p></div> </div> </p> </section> </body> </html>
