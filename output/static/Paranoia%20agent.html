She emerges from the huge delusion, now represented as a huge dark goo/fog, which becomes the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Sea.html">Sea</a> (the sea represents chaos, like in many old cosmogenic <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Myth.html">Myth</a>s). And she floats to the surface, as if becoming a new person, and I think making a reference to the very similar scene in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="Ghost%2520in%2520the%2520shell.html">Ghost in the shell</a> (1995 movie).</p><p>Cool weird intro with everyone laughing..</p><p>Maromi is Shonnen Bato</p><p>Shonnen Bato turns out to be just any excuse we put to escape reality. It is the reassuring fable, which we use, instead of confronting the truth, which may be harsh/difficult.</p><p><a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://the-artifice.com/satoshi-kon-otaku-anime/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://the-artifice.com/satoshi-kon-otaku-anime/</a></p><hr><p>&quot;At times, the series resembles an anthology, with each episode throwing its star character through a Twist Ending — but everything later becomes connected in a way that rivals Serial Experiments Lain's levels of conspiracy and surrealism. &quot; ( <a class="tc-tiddlylink-external" href="https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Anime/ParanoiaAgent" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Anime/ParanoiaAgent</a> )</p><p>&quot; *Giving into lies and illusions to escape the hardships of reality might feel good in the short term, but it can't last.
