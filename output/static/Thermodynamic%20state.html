<p><sub><em>also known as Gibbs states, Gibbs equi-librium measures, or simply Gibbs measures</em></sub></p><p>Thermodynamic states characterize the macroscopic state of many-body systems (see <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Bulk%20matter">Bulk matter</a>). It may be defined as corresponding to a particular choice of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Thermodynamic%20potential">Thermodynamic potential</a> parameters, that in turn, determine a particular <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Probability%20distribution">Probability distribution</a> over <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-missing" href="#Microstate">Microstate</a>s, as determined by <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Statistical%20physics">Statistical physics</a>.</p><p>The above, more specifically, defines a <em>pure thermodynamic state</em>. </p>
