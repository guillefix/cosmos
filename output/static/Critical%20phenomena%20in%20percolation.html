<p><a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Critical%20phenomena">Critical phenomena</a> in <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Percolation">Percolation</a> occurs at the critical value of the occupation probability corresponding to the <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Percolation%20phase%20transition">Percolation phase transition</a>, which separates the percolating and the non-percolating phases.</p><h3 class=""><u>Critical phenomena</u></h3><p>Percolation models at the critical point show several interesting critical phenomena:</p><ul><li><strong>Symmetries</strong><ul><li>Scaling invariance.</li><li>Conformal invariance. This has lead to modelling them (as in many other examples of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Critical%20phenomena">Critical phenomena</a>) in the continuum limit, as a <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Conformal%20field%20theory">Conformal field theory</a>.</li></ul></li><li><u>Fractal structure of the critical percolation clusters</u>. Scaling invariance leads to the self-similarity characteristic of <a class="tc-tiddlylink tc-tiddlylink-resolves" href="#Fractal">Fractal</a>s, and indeed the clusters have fractal geometry.
